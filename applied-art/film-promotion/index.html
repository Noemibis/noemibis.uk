Through the door

The scariest and inventive movie is back! Despite everything, this time it's the monsters who are scared silly, by a little and sweet girl named Boo.

Where the world is opposite and monsters fear humans, Disney-Pixar is able to give the right meaning to the story. There's a reason why there are monsters in children's closets—it's their job. Monsters, Inc. is the most successful scream-processing factory in the monster world, and there is no better Scarer than James P. Sullivan. But when "Sulley" accidentally lets a little human girl into Monstropolis, life turns upside down for him and his buddy Mike.

There hasn't been a film in years to use creative energy as efficiently as Monsters, Inc.

One of those rare family films that parents can enjoy (rather than endure) along with their kids!
